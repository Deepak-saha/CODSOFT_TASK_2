ABOUT US

We are a collective of seasoned individuals with decades of expertise in cultivating and distributing mangoes. Our profound understanding of what defines an exceptional mango extends to the intricacies of their growth. This tropical gem thrives in regions with a warm climate, well-draining loamy soil enriched with organic matter, and optimal conditions for proper drainage and aeration. Nestled in the heart of India, our orchards benefit from the bountiful sunshine and fertile soil, creating an environment that is ideal for cultivating the finest mango varieties. The tropical paradise of our location, is celebrated globally for its rich and flavorful mangoes. The key influencers for the thriving mango trees are the soil quality, climate nuances, and the overall environment, all of which converge harmoniously in our region. Each mango from our orchards boasts a distinctive appearance, taste, aroma, size, and color, making them a truly unparalleled delight for mango enthusiasts around the world.

VARIETIES

Alphonso Mangoes

Mid-July unveils the golden delight of Alphonso Mangoes, cherished globally for their exquisite taste. Ratnagiri, Maharashtra, stands as the exclusive hub for these regal mangoes.

kesar mangoes

Kesar mangoes owe their name to their saffron appearance and heavenly taste, distinct sweet flavour, is considered to be the 'Queen of Mangoes'.

Dasheri Mangoes

The land of Nawabs is equally famous for its royal mangoes. Lucknow, along with its adjoining towns, is widely known for its mango belt of North India.

Himsagar and Kishan Bhog Mangoes

One of the Nawabi cities of West Bengal, the city of Murshidabad, is famous for its vast variety of delicious mangoes.

Chausa Mangoes

Hardoi in Uttar Pradesh is another hotspot for mango-lovers.these mangoes fall under the 'sucking' category and are exclusively famous for their yellowish colour and aroma.

Raspuri Mangoes

Predominantly cultivated in the southern state of Karnataka,Raspuri Mangoes are an extremely popular variety of mangoes. Distinguished by its oval shape and its skin which is almost inedible
